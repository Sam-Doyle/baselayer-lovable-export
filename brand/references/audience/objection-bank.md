# Objection Bank

Top 10 objections from ICP research, ranked by frequency. Each includes a ready-to-use copy example for ad, email, and product description skills.

---

## Objection 1: "I don't need skincare"
- **Frequency:** Most common. ~60% of men report "not interested" in skincare. 58% rarely or never moisturize.
- **Rebuttal approach:** Health framing + daily habit analogy. Don't sell skincare — sell maintenance.
- **Proof point:** Shaving strips the outer skin layer. Daily SPF use lowers visible aging signs by 24%. Skin cancer is the most common cancer in the US.
- **Copy example:** "You brush your teeth every morning. This is the same thing for your face. 15 seconds. Done."

## Objection 2: "Skincare is for women"
- **Frequency:** High, especially 35+. 73% of men 51+ would not consider cosmetics vs. only 37% of men 18-34.
- **Rebuttal approach:** Normalization with statistics + reframing as maintenance, not beauty.
- **Proof point:** 52% of US men now use facial skincare — 68% increase since 2022. 68% of Gen Z men use it.
- **Copy example:** "52% of men already use skincare. You wouldn't skip the gym because 'exercise is feminine.' This is maintenance, not makeup."

## Objection 3: "It's too complicated"
- **Frequency:** High. Complexity is the #1 barrier to starting. Men are 2x more likely than women to feel lost about products.
- **Rebuttal approach:** Simplicity proof — one product replaces four.
- **Proof point:** Base Layer replaces moisturizer + sunscreen + serum + treatment in a single step. 15 seconds.
- **Copy example:** "You don't need 10 products, fancy rollers, or a 20-minute ritual. One product. One step. That's it."

## Objection 4: "It's too expensive / waste of money"
- **Frequency:** Moderate-high. Only 2% of men would spend $60+ on a single product. $20-40 is the sweet spot.
- **Rebuttal approach:** Value math + cost-of-inaction comparison.
- **Proof point:** $38 = $1.27/day (less than coffee). Replaces $54+/month in separate products. Laser treatments for sun damage cost $200-2,000 per session.
- **Copy example:** "$1.27 a day. Less than your coffee. Replaces your moisturizer, sunscreen, serum, and treatment. Do the math."

## Objection 5: "I'll just use my girlfriend's products"
- **Frequency:** Moderate. Common entry point — partners introduce skincare, men borrow products.
- **Rebuttal approach:** Science differentiation — men's skin has genuinely different needs.
- **Proof point:** Men's skin is 20-25% thicker, produces more sebum, has larger pores. Women's products target different skin characteristics.
- **Copy example:** "Her moisturizer wasn't built for your skin. Men produce more oil, have thicker skin, and need different ingredients. This was engineered for how your skin actually works."

## Objection 6: "I've never seen results from skincare"
- **Frequency:** Moderate. Often from men who tried a random product once and quit.
- **Rebuttal approach:** Set realistic timelines + remove risk with guarantee.
- **Proof point:** 84% of men noticed improved skin clarity within 4 weeks. Niacinamide shows visible pore reduction in 2-4 weeks. 30-day money-back guarantee.
- **Copy example:** "84% of men see results in 4 weeks. If you don't, keep the bottle and get your money back. Zero risk."

## Objection 7: "I don't want something greasy or heavy"
- **Frequency:** Moderate-high. "Greasy residue" is a top complaint about existing products.
- **Rebuttal approach:** Texture proof — show, don't tell. UGC of application + matte finish.
- **Proof point:** Absorbs in 15 seconds. Hyaluronic acid + niacinamide deliver lightweight hydration with a matte finish. Zero residue.
- **Copy example:** "Absorbs in 15 seconds. Matte finish. Zero grease. If your face feels like a frying pan 30 minutes later, you're using the wrong product."

## Objection 8: "I don't trust skincare brands — they're all hype"
- **Frequency:** Moderate. 42% of male consumers now check ingredient labels before purchase.
- **Rebuttal approach:** Full ingredient transparency. No marketing jargon — just what's in it and what it does.
- **Proof point:** Niacinamide (oil control), Hyaluronic Acid (hydration), Copper Peptide GHK-Cu (anti-aging), SPF 30 (UV protection). No filler. No fragrance. No paid endorsements.
- **Copy example:** "We don't do influencer deals or 'clinically proven' claims. Here's exactly what's in it, exactly what each ingredient does, and a money-back guarantee. Check the label yourself."

## Objection 9: "My skin is fine — I've never used anything and it's been fine"
- **Frequency:** Moderate. Common in men under 30 whose damage hasn't surfaced yet.
- **Rebuttal approach:** The "invisible damage" angle — sun damage is cumulative and hidden until it's too late.
- **Proof point:** By the time wrinkles appear, 80% of the damage is already done. Skin starts showing visible aging in your 30s. Prevention is 1/100th the cost of treatment.
- **Copy example:** "Your skin looks fine now. But sun damage is invisible until your 30s — and by then, 80% of the damage is done. This isn't a fix. It's prevention."

## Objection 10: "I don't want to get locked into a subscription"
- **Frequency:** Moderate and rising. Competitors like Lumin face heavy backlash for aggressive, hard-to-cancel subscriptions.
- **Rebuttal approach:** Anti-subscription transparency. Lead with one-time purchase. No dark patterns.
- **Proof point:** Base Layer is a single purchase at $38. No auto-renewals. No tricks. Optional subscription only if you want it, cancel with one click.
- **Copy example:** "No subscription traps. No 'cancel in 3 business days' nonsense. Buy once. If you want more, come back. We respect you enough to let you leave."
