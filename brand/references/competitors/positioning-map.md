## White Space We Own

1. **Single SKU simplicity** -- Most competitors sell systems/routines. Only Cardon launched single-SKU (and has since expanded). "One product, everything you need" is defensible.
2. **Clinical actives at accessible-premium price** -- $38 sits above mass ($7-15) and below luxury ($45+). Niacinamide + HA + Vitamin C + SPF 30 outperforms competitors at this price.
3. **SPF 30 as standard** -- Jack Black is SPF 20. Harry's is SPF 15. Many competitors have no SPF. SPF 30 is the dermatologist-recommended minimum.
4. **Anti-subscription trust** -- Lumin, Tiege, and Geologie all face subscription backlash. Transparent one-time purchase builds trust with skeptical men.
5. **Performance positioning** -- Not "luxury for men" (Brickell), not "budget basics" (Bulldog/Harry's), not "K-beauty" (Cardon). PERFORMANCE. Like athletic wear for your face.
6. **Altitude credibility** -- Formulated in Breckenridge at 9,600 ft. The altitude is a proving ground, not a gimmick. No competitor has an origin story this specific.
7. **Founder-led authenticity** -- Tiege was built on influencers. 2026 data shows founder-led creative gets 2.3x engagement and 41% lower CPA vs. generic UGC.
