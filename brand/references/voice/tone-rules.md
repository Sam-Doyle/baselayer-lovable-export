# Base Layer — Voice & Tone Rules

**Source:** BASE_LAYER_BRAND_GUIDELINES.md Sections 7, 12
**Use:** Any skill that writes text for the brand.

---

## Brand Voice: "Your Sharp Friend"

Direct, confident, conversational. Like a friend who happens to know about skincare but doesn't make it weird.

## Voice Characteristics

| Attribute | We Are | We Are NOT |
|---|---|---|
| Direct | "One step. Done." | "Explore our carefully curated range" |
| Confident | "The product works." | "We believe this could help" |
| Honest | "50 early testers. Zero refund requests." | "Clinically proven by dermatologists" |
| Conversational | "That greasy feeling? Gone." | "Eliminate excess sebum production" |
| Masculine-neutral | "Look sharper" | "Anti-aging miracle for him" |
| Anti-complexity | "Everything your skin needs. Nothing it doesn't." | "Advanced 6-step regimen" |

## Sentence Style Rules

- Short, punchy, declarative.
- Period-terminated or no punctuation. Never question marks in headlines.
- Lead with benefits, not features.
- Use real numbers ("15 seconds", "50 testers", "$38").
- State facts. Never "we think" or "we believe."
- Headings are ALWAYS uppercase.

## Banned Words

Do not use these words in any brand copy:
- curated
- elevated
- artisanal
- lifestyle (as a buzzword)
- we think
- we believe
- clinically proven (unless backed by actual clinical trial)
- miracle
- anti-aging miracle
- advanced regimen
- explore (as CTA verb)
- carefully
- luxurious / luxury
- indulge / pampering
- any fragrance/scent language (the product is fragrance-free)

## Banned Punctuation & Formatting

- No emojis on any brand touchpoint.
- No exclamation marks in headlines.
- No long-form marketing copy in headings.
- No question marks in headlines (rhetorical questions OK in body copy).

## Copy Do's

- Use uppercase for all headings.
- Keep copy short, punchy, declarative.
- Lead with benefits, not features.
- Use real numbers and specifics.
- Reference Colorado/altitude as a credibility anchor.
- Speak to real frustrations — be specific, be empathetic, but not soft.

## Copy Don'ts

- Don't use color for decoration in copy (visual rule, but affects badges/highlights).
- Don't write long-form marketing copy in headings.
- Don't say "we think" or "we believe" — state facts.
- Don't use lifestyle buzzwords (curated, elevated, artisanal).
- Don't add fragrance/scent language to copy.
- Don't reference competitors by name.
- Don't add emojis to any brand touchpoint.
- Don't use exclamation marks in headlines.
